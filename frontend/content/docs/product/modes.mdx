# Modes & Reasoning

The composer toolbar exposes three independent controls that influence how
Pawrrtal handles your next message: plan mode, safety mode, and reasoning level.
All three are persisted to `localStorage` so your choices survive across page
loads.

## Plan mode

**Plan mode** adds a planning step to the request. The toggle is labelled
**Plan** in the toolbar (with a list-checks icon). You can also show or hide
the Plan button with the keyboard shortcut **Shift+Tab**.

Use plan mode when you want the model to outline its approach before executing.
This is useful for multi-step tasks — refactoring a module, writing a long
document, or debugging a complex problem — where seeing the plan first lets you
redirect before the model commits to a direction.

Plan mode visibility is stored in `localStorage` under the key
`chat-composer:plan-mode-visible` and defaults to hidden for new sessions.

## Safety mode

**Safety mode** controls what permissions the agent has when executing tools and
code. The selector is in the composer toolbar, to the right of the Plan button.
Four modes are available:

- **Default permissions** — the baseline set of capabilities; shown with a blue
  hand icon.
- **Auto-review** *(default)* — the model flags changes for human review before
  applying them; shown with a green shield-check icon.
- **Full access** — the model can apply changes without review; shown with a red
  shield-alert icon. This is an advanced mode and is separated from the two
  above by a divider in the dropdown.
- **Custom (config.toml)** — loads permissions from your local `config.toml`
  file; also in the advanced section.

The active mode is stored in `localStorage` under the key
`chat-composer:safety-mode`. The default is **Auto-review**.

When in doubt, stay on **Auto-review**. Switch to **Default permissions** for
read-only or exploratory sessions where you just want answers without the model
touching anything. Reserve **Full access** for tasks where you have already
reviewed the plan and want uninterrupted execution.

## Reasoning level

**Reasoning level** controls how much extended chain-of-thought the model uses
before producing its response. The selector lives inside the model selector
popover — click the composer's model/reasoning button to open it, then choose
the **Thinking** row at the bottom of the menu.

Four levels are available: **Low**, **Medium**, **High**, and **Extra High**.

The active level is persisted under `chat-composer:selected-reasoning-level`
and defaults to **Medium**.

Higher reasoning levels produce more considered responses but take longer and
consume more tokens. Use **Low** for quick factual questions, **Medium** for
most everyday tasks, **High** for hard problems that benefit from deliberation,
and **Extra High** for tasks where thoroughness matters more than speed.
